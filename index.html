The one that mattered most
A wearable that turns sign language into speech, in real time.

My cousin is deaf. Most of the world isn't built to hear her. So I built a bridge: flex sensors down each finger, an IMU on the back of the hand, a trained gesture classifier, and an onboard speaker — no app, no phone, no second person required.

She signs. It speaks. The classifier runs on embedded C++, trained on a custom gesture dataset I collected and labelled myself.

"I didn't build this for a judging panel. I built it because watching someone you love wait for a translator is a problem with a solvable cause."
