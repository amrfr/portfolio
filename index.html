Personal Build · Not for a competition

The Sign Language Glove

My cousin is deaf. Most of our family learned some signs over the years, but it never felt like enough — too many conversations still needed someone to translate in the middle. So I built her a glove that speaks for her: flex sensors down each finger and an IMU on the back of the hand read her gestures in real time, a trained classifier matches the motion to a sign, and a small onboard speaker says the word out loud the moment she signs it.

No app to open, no phone to hold up — just the glove, on her hand, talking for her. It's the project I'm proudest of, not because it won anything, but because it didn't have to.

"I didn't build it to impress a judge. I built it so my cousin doesn't have to wait for someone to translate her."

① Role Model
✦
Historical figure · 1452 – 1519
Leonardo da Vinci
Engineer · Artist · Scientist · Architect · Anatomist · Inventor

Most people remember Leonardo as the man who painted the Mona Lisa. I remember him as the man who designed a functioning helicopter 400 years before one was built, mapped the human cardiovascular system without a microscope, engineered hydraulic machines, and still found time to redefine what a portrait could be. He didn't choose between engineering and art — he refused the question entirely.

That's what draws me to him. Not the genius — the refusal to be one thing. In an era that rewards narrow specialisation, Leonardo is proof that the most important breakthroughs happen at the intersection of disciplines nobody thought to combine. I build embedded systems and close sponsorship deals. I write firmware and read financial reports. I don't think those are in tension — Leonardo showed me they're the same instinct wearing different clothes.
